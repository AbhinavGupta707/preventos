You can get help right now.

Everything on this page is free, confidential, and open at this hour — including the middle of the night.

Emergency — 999 24/7 · if you or someone else is in immediate danger

If you can't speak safely, dial 999, then press 55 when prompted.

Call 999
Samaritans — 116 123 24/7 · free · whatever you're going through

Someone will listen, without judgement, for as long as you need.

Call 116 123
Shout — text 85258 24/7 · free · if talking feels too hard right now

Text SHOUT to 85258 to message with a trained volunteer.

Text SHOUT to 85258
NHS 111 24/7 · urgent medical advice that isn't a 999 emergency

Including urgent mental health support — say you need help with your mental health.

Call 111

If someone is hurting you

National Domestic Abuse Helpline — 0808 2000 247 24/7 · free · run by Refuge

Confidential support, whoever you are, whatever the situation.

Call 0808 2000 247

If you're worried about a child — or you're under 19

NSPCC — 0808 800 5000 free · if you're worried about a child, however unsure Call 0808 800 5000
Childline — 0800 1111 24/7 · free · for anyone under 19, about anything Call 0800 1111

Alcohol

If your body has become used to alcohol, do not suddenly stop drinking on your own — stopping abruptly can be dangerous. Shaking, sweating, fits, or seeing things after stopping is a medical emergency: call 999.
Drinkline — 0300 123 1110 weekdays 9am–8pm, weekends 11am–4pm · free Call 0300 123 1110
